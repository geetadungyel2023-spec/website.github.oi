Welcome

Welcome to the Scouting in Bhutan website! This site is dedicated to sharing the rich history, core values, and leadership training of the Scout movement both globally and within Bhutan.

The author of this website is Geeta Dungyel, an avid scout and community member passionate about youth development and outdoor education. Geeta has participated in numerous scouting activities and training, and aims to inspire new generations to embrace the ideals of scouting.

History of Scouting

The Scout movement was founded by Robert Baden-Powell in 1907 in England. It started as a way to teach young boys survival skills, character, and leadership through outdoor activities and community service. Over the years, scouting has grown into a global movement with millions of members dedicated to making the world a better place through service, leadership, and personal development.

Scout Promise

On my honour,
I promise that I will do my best,
To do my duty to God,
Duty to tsa-wa-sum (King, Country and People),
Duty to self, parents, others
and to obey the Scout Law

Motto

"Be Prepared"

The Scout motto encourages members to always be ready to face challenges and help others, embodying the spirit of preparedness and service.

Scout Law

  • A Scout is trustworthy.
  • A Scout is loyal.
  • A Scout is helpful.
  • A Scout is friendly and considerate.
  • A Scout is kind.
  • A Scout is obedient.
  • A Scout is cheerful.
  • A Scout is thrifty.
  • A Scout is brave.
  • A Scout is clean in thought, word, and deed.

Scout Anthem

*The Scout Song* (example lyrics)
"On my honour I promise true, To do my best for you, To be honest, kind, and brave, And help others every day."

Leadership Training

Leadership training in scouting develops skills such as teamwork, responsibility, decision-making, and communication. Through activities like camping, community service projects, and team challenges, scouts learn to lead by example and serve as positive role models.
